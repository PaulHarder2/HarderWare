# WX-230 — partial trailing day no longer fakes a temperature cooldown

**Type:** Functional verification (post-deploy), **manual spot-check**. The fix is deterministic and unit-test-proven (`TemperatureRangeSummarizerTests`: `DailyHighsLows_PartialTrailingDay_ExcludedFromHighs_KeptForLows`, `Characterize_SteadyHeatWithPartialPreDawnLastDay_NoPhantomCooldown`); this confirms it live. No scripted fingerprint — "absence of a phantom cooldown" is not cleanly DB-greppable, so this is an eyeball check.

**Release:** 1.38.3
**Components:** WxReportSvc — `TemperatureRangeSummarizer.DailyHighsLows`

## Background

The WX-228 temperature summary split daytime highs into an early/later pair whenever the per-day highs varied ≥ 3 °C. Every report's GFS horizon ends on a **partial trailing local day** that has only its pre-dawn/morning block — its "high" is a pre-dawn temperature, not a daytime maximum. That bogus low high made a steady-heat week (e.g. 98–99 °F every afternoon) split off a phantom "cooldown later" (the 1.38.2 diagnostic read "highs … ease back toward 81–84 °F … a modest step down" off a Jul-3 pre-dawn 28.4 °C).

Fix (symmetric, gating each extreme on the band that holds it): a day feeds the **highs** series only if it has its **afternoon (local 12:00–18:00)** block, and the **lows** series only if it has a **pre-dawn or morning (local 00:00 or 06:00)** block. This also covers the mirror case — a leading day (today, sent past the morning) whose earliest block is the afternoon has only daytime minima, not a real overnight low, so it no longer contributes a bogus low.

## Preconditions

- 1.38.3 deployed (`deploy-history.log` has a `WxReportSvc 1.38.3` line).
- A delivered report covering a **steady-heat multi-day stretch** (the common Houston-summer case) whose forecast horizon ends on a partial (pre-dawn/morning-only) last local day — i.e. essentially any normal report.

## Procedure

1. **Read a recent steady-heat report's closing** (or the structured-report narrative).
   - **Expect:** the daytime-highs summary reads as a **single band** — e.g. "highs near 97–100 °F all week" — with **no** spurious "ease back / cooling later / clouds build" split that is really just the partial last day. A genuine multi-day trend (a real front) may still legitimately split; the test is that a *flat-afternoon* week does **not**.

2. **(Deeper, optional) Inspect the snapshot of a report whose last local day is pre-dawn-only.** Pull `ForecastSnapshots.Body` for the send and confirm the daily afternoon (local 12:00) highs are steady and the trailing pre-dawn-only day is **not** represented in the highs summary; the lows summary still spans all days.
   - **Cross-check vs the grid:** the Extended Forecast **grid** may still show that partial last day's low pseudo-high in its final row — that is pre-existing and **out of scope** here (flagged in the ticket). The *summary* must no longer treat it as a high.
