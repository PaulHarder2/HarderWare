# WX-157 — post-deploy verification (pre-scheduled quiet window + late-scheduled fix)

WX-157 makes the scheduled report take precedence and adds a **day-banded,
severity-aware pre-scheduled quiet window** on *unscheduled* updates:

1. **Late-scheduled fix** — `ShouldSend` now evaluates a due scheduled slot **before**
   the `MinGapMinutes` gap, so a scheduled report fires on its slot and is never
   deferred by a preceding unscheduled send (a "7 AM report" arrives at 7, not up to
   90 min later).
2. **Quiet window** — a significant, non-severe-onset unscheduled change is suppressed
   when the next scheduled slot falls within the change's day-band quiet window
   (`PreScheduledQuietSchedule` `1:90,3:180` → a near-term change is quieted in the
   90 min before the slot, a day-3+ change in the 180 min before it). Its content rides
   the upcoming scheduled report (which renders "What's changed").
3. **Severe punch-through** — a not-severe→severe onset (`SevereEntered`) sends
   immediately even inside the quiet window; the 90-minute `MinGapMinutes` remains a
   hard floor.

It also normalizes WX-181's `UpdateDebounceSchedule` to minutes (`1:360,3:720` —
identical durations) under one shared `DayBandedSchedule`; WX-181's behavior and its
verify fingerprints are unchanged.
