# WX-244 — Free narrative uses a "night" word for the evening/00-06 daypart and crosses the day boundary

**Bug · verifies the free narrative honors the day-bound daypart discipline (never "`<day>` night"; evening → the approved evening word; 00-06 → the early-morning-of-its-own-day framing).**

## The bug

The free-composed narrative (`changeSummary` / `closing`) rendered the **evening** daypart with a **night** word in es — English "Tuesday evening" → "el martes por la tarde y **la noche**", and even "hasta **la noche del martes**" (Tuesday night). In the WxServices daypart model, "`<day>` night" reads as the night *following* that day (the next day's 00-06 wee hours), so it mislabels the day. The deterministic grid/obs were already correct (WX-190 binds 00-06 to its day via clock ranges, and es `PartEvening` = "Tarde-Noche" after Paul's Copy→DB); only the **free narrative** lacked the discipline.

## The fix (one layer — prompt)

- **Anchor the named dayparts.** `PartMorning`/`PartAfternoon`/`PartEvening` added to the `PromptGlossaryTokens` registry, so `NarrativeGlossary` injects the approved daypart word per language (es evening = "Tarde-Noche") — sourced from `LanguageTemplates`, DRY-clean.
- **Time-of-day discipline in the reconciler system prompt:** never attach a night-word to a day name; evening → the approved word; the 00-06 block is the **early-morning hours of its own day** ("la madrugada del sábado") — a clock band, not sunrise-relative ("pre-dawn"), distinct from the 06-12 morning, never "`<prior day>` night".

No deterministic post-check (layer 2) — added only if this re-run shows the prompt alone doesn't hold.
