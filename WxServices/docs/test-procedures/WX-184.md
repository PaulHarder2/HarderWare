# WX-184 — Report clarity: obs-time, relabel, fallback-prose suppression, legend reposition

**Type:** Manual functional verification (post-deploy). No verify script — the change is visual, confirmed by reading a rendered report.

**Release:** 1.36.0
**Components:** WxReport.Svc (renderer + reconciler), MetarParser.Data (`RelabelCurrentToReportedConditions` migration)

## Preconditions

- 1.36.0 deployed; the `RelabelCurrentToReportedConditions` migration applied at startup (confirm via the service log or an `__EFMigrationsHistory` row).
- At least one enabled recipient in English and one in another enabled language (es / de / da / eo).

## Procedure

1. Trigger or wait for a scheduled report to a recipient in **English** (or open the most recent committed send's `EmailBody`).
   - **Expect:** the latest-observation section heading reads **"Reported Conditions"** (not "Current Conditions").
   - **Expect:** beneath the heading, the station/observation line shows the observation's **local date + time** (e.g. "at Houston Executive Airport · Sat, Jun 14, 11:15 PM"); when the observing station equals the locality, the time shows on its own.
   - **Expect:** the 24-hour-clock legend ("Times use a 24-hour clock…") appears **above** the forecast grid, immediately beneath the "Forecast for …" heading rule, **left-justified** — not centered below the grid.

2. Repeat step 1 for a recipient in a **non-English** enabled language (e.g. es).
   - **Expect:** the heading is the localized "Reported Conditions" — es: "Condiciones reportadas"; de: "Gemeldete Wetterlage"; da: "Rapporterede forhold"; eo: "Raportitaj Kondiĉoj".
   - **Expect:** legend repositioned and observation time present, as in step 1.

3. Confirm the fallback narration is gone. Either (a) inspect a report produced while the primary station is unavailable and a fallback station is in use (e.g. the current KDWH outage), or (b) review the reconciler output.
   - **Expect:** neither the summary/closing nor the "What's changed" band contains any sentence narrating the data source / fallback (no "routine fallback behavior, nothing to be concerned about", no "nearest station with current data"). The station is conveyed only by the structured "at <Station>" line.
   - **Expect (log):** when a station fallback occurs, the WxReport log still records `METAR station changed X → Y (fallback station in use).`
