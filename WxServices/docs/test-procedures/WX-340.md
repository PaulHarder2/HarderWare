# WX-340 — Functional test procedure (production §13 watch)

**Ticket:** WX-340 (Subtask of Story WX-331, under Epic WX-137) — drop the per-language deterministic
prose checks (word-bags + both-days) and carry their classes in the reconciler prompt uniformly for every
language; fold in the WX-239 day-part-endpoint inclusive-span rule.

**Release under test:** v1.60.3 · **Component:** `WxReport.Svc` (the reconciler).

## Why this is a production watch, not a unit test

The change *removes* the deterministic validators that formerly rejected four prose classes and moves
their coverage entirely into the reconciler generation prompt. A stubbed-Claude unit test cannot exercise
the real prompt's effect on the live model, so the only meaningful verification is reading **rendered
production prose across real cycles** and confirming the strengthened prompt + the WX-189 retry/section-
degrade machinery prevent or repair each class. The unit suite proves only that the survivor
(`{q:time}`↔day-part) and the kept hygiene regexes still fire, and that nothing else regressed.

The four dropped classes and the fold-in, each now carried by an explicit prompt rule:

| Class (retired check) | What must NOT appear in rendered prose |
|---|---|
| precip-at-a-dry-time (WX-152 `CheckClosingSentence`) | a precip/storm assertion at a local time the snapshot leaves entirely dry |
| aggregate-dry (WX-177 `CheckAggregateDryClaim`) | an aggregate period (weekend/week) called dry while a day within it is wet |
| severe-storms register (WX-284/293 `CheckSevereStormVocabulary`) | "severe storms" wording for a window with no severe **convective** block; a non-severe thunderstorm rendered as anything but "rain" |
| cross-midnight both-days (WX-264) | a midnight-crossing window named by its tail day only (e.g. "the early hours of Tuesday" dropping the Monday-evening start) |
| WX-239 fold-in (day-part span) | German bare `bis [zum] [weekday]abend` (or es literal `hasta [día]`) that truncates a "through [day] evening/afternoon" span short of the grid's endpoint |

## Boundary

Post-deploy window begins at the `WxReport.Svc` v1.60.3 deploy time (read from `deploy-history.log`).
Compare rendered prose in the window against the classes above. There is **no verify script** — the
signal is qualitative (reading rendered reports + the Gemini QA-judge packages), not a log fingerprint,
because the retired checks no longer emit degrade lines and the prompt's success is the *absence* of the
bad prose, judged natively.

## Procedure

1. **Confirm the deploy.** In `deploy-history.log`, find the most recent `WxReport.Svc` line at version
   `1.60.3` (+ its commit). *Expect:* one present; note its timestamp as the window start.

2. **Baseline smoke.** Confirm `WxReport.Svc` came up cleanly after the deploy: services up, heartbeat
   current, no new ERRORs in `wxreport-svc.log` beyond the pre-deploy background. *Expect:* healthy; the
   removed validators mean the `ValidateClosingClaims` / `CheckSevereStormVocabulary` degrade lines should
   simply **stop appearing** (their absence is expected, not a signal).

3. **Read rendered prose across cycles (the core watch).** Over an active window — enough cycles to span
   varied weather (a target of **≥ 10 scheduled/unscheduled sends** across en + the non-English recipients,
   spanning at least one wet day and one severe/convective day) — read the rendered reports (or rerun
   Translation QA) and check each of the five rows above. *Expect:* none of the bad-prose classes appear;
   where the model drifts, the WX-189 retry/section-degrade repairs it before send (a dropped section is a
   repair, not a failure).

4. **Exercise the severe-convective distinction specifically.** On a cycle carrying a severe **convective**
   block, confirm "severe storms" wording is used; on a cycle with a non-severe thunderstorm block, confirm
   the prose says plain "rain". *Expect:* the rain-vs-severe-storms binary holds.

5. **Exercise the WX-239 day-part span specifically.** On a German (and, opportunistically, Spanish) cycle
   whose grid carries a "through [weekday] evening/afternoon" span, read the rendered span. *Expect:* the
   inclusive form (`bis einschließlich …abend` or the enumerated `…nachmittag und -abend`), never a bare
   `bis [weekday]abend` that a reader could take as ending ~18:00.

6. **Cross-check with the Gemini QA judge.** For the non-English recipients (de/es/eo/da), run/read the
   Translation-QA judge output over the window and confirm it does not flag the classes above. *Expect:*
   no new native-judge findings tied to the dropped classes.

## Verdict

- **PASS** — across the window, none of the five classes reach a recipient (prevented by the prompt or
  repaired by the WX-189 degrade), the severe-convective binary holds, and the WX-239 span renders
  inclusively. Record `PASS yyyy-mm-dd @ v1.60.3` in the ticket's **Test Result**.
- **FAIL** — a class recurs in shipped prose and is not repaired. Record `FAIL …`, capture the offending
  rendered sentence(s), and open a follow-up to tighten the specific prompt rule (fix-forward; the
  deterministic check is not coming back — that is the ticket's deliberate design).
