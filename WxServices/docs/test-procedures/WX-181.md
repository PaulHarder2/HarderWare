# WX-181 — post-deploy verification (day-banded update debounce)

WX-181 added a **day-banded, severity-aware debounce** on *unscheduled* updates. A
significant but non-severe-onset change is suppressed when its day-band's minimum gap
has not elapsed since the last unscheduled send. Schedule `1:6,3:12` → a change
touching day 1–2 (today/tomorrow) needs a 6 h gap; a change only reaching day 3+ needs
12 h. A not-severe→severe onset **punches through** regardless, and the service-wide
90-minute `MinGapMinutes` remains a hard floor beneath all of it. This trims the
residual update-churn cost left after WX-180's clamp fix.

- **Fingerprint (expect):** `WX-181 debounce suppressed …` (enforce) and/or

- **Failure signature → FAIL:** any `invalid UpdateDebounceSchedule` line — the

- **Context (not gating):** scheduled reports still generated (`generating scheduled
  report`) — the debounce is unscheduled-only, so cadence must persist.

## Manual confirmation

Two acceptance points the log fingerprint can't assert on its own:

1. **Severe punch-through** — a not-severe→severe onset must still send immediately
   even inside a band's quiet window. Confirm against the most recent severe-onset
   delivery (recipient received it without the band-gap delay), or exercise it
   deliberately when a severe forecast next occurs.
2. **Cost trend** — daily Claude `$`-rate should trend **down** from the post-WX-180
   baseline on the **Grafana WxReport cost panel** once the averaging window clears.
