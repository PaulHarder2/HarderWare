# WX-223 — Meteogram caption + alt text localization (Phase A of WX-221)

**Type:** Manual functional verification (post-deploy). No verify script — the caption is visible below the chart and the alt text is read from the email source.

**Release:** 1.37.0
**Components:** WxReport.Svc (ReportWorker), MetarParser.Data (`AddMeteogramCaptionTokens` migration)

## Preconditions

- 1.37.0 deployed; the `AddMeteogramCaptionTokens` migration applied at startup (confirm via the service log or an `__EFMigrationsHistory` row).
- At least one enabled recipient in English and one in another enabled language (es / de / da / eo), each with a meteogram available for their locality.

## Procedure

1. Trigger or wait for a report to a recipient in **English** that includes a meteogram (or open the most recent committed send's `EmailBody` and the delivered email).
   - **Expect:** the italic caption beneath the chart reads "Forecast of temperature, humidity, and wind over time. Wind symbols point in the direction the wind is blowing, with more feathers indicating stronger winds."
   - **Expect:** the `<img>` `alt` attribute is "48-hour forecast meteogram" (inspect the HTML source).

2. Repeat for a recipient in a **non-English** enabled language (e.g. es).
   - **Expect:** the caption is the localized phrase — es: "Pronóstico de temperatura, humedad y viento a lo largo del tiempo. …"; the alt attribute is the localized alt (es: "Meteograma de pronóstico de 48 horas").
   - **Note:** the **in-image** chart labels (Wind / RH / temperature axis / day-of-week ticks) are still in English at this phase — that is expected; they are localized in WX-224 (Phase B), not here.

Record in the Jira **Test Result** field: `PASS yyyy-mm-dd @ 1.37.0` (or `FAIL …`).

## Notes

- The de / da / eo caption + alt translations are Claude-generated; an independent / native back-check is tracked under WX-214. A reasonable-but-imperfect translation is not a FAIL of this subtask's scope.
